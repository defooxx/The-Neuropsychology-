# Reductionism vs Holism

Another important debate in psychology is whether mental phenomena can be fully explained by biology.

Reductionism

Reductionism argues that complex mental processes can be explained by examining their smallest parts.

Examples of components studied include:
	•	DNA
	•	genes
	•	proteins
	•	neurons
	•	brain circuits

In extreme reductionism, consciousness might be explained as:

“nothing more than neurons firing and chemicals flowing in the brain.”

⸻

# Holism (Against Reductionism)

Holism argues that some truths only appear at higher levels of organization.

In this view:

The whole is greater than the sum of its parts.

For example, individual neurons do not contain consciousness but when billions of neurons interact, conscious awareness emerges.

⸻

# Mental States vs Brain States

Neuropsychology tries to understand the relationship between:
	•	Mental states — thoughts, emotions, perceptions
	•	Brain states — neural activity in the brain

Modern tools such as neuroimaging allow scientists to observe brain activity during different mental processes.

This research helps reveal how physical brain activity relates to psychological experience.
